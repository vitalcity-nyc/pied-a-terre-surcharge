Five questions the rules still have to answer

The statute leaves much to the Department of Finance's rulemaking. How these are resolved will shape who actually pays.

1?
Co-op valuation

How will Finance value an individual co-op apartment in phase two?

Directly, from its own comparable sales — or by splitting a building-wide value the same imperfect way as phase one, which would simply re-create the allocation problem on a bigger number.

2?
Changing use

What happens to a home bought but not yet occupied while it is renovated?

Or to a home whose use changes mid-year? The law does not clearly say.

3?
Mixed-use buildings

How are mixed-use co-ops handled?

Where valuable ground-floor retail inflates the building value that gets spread across the residential apartments above it.

4?
Schedule

Will Finance commit to a predictable schedule?

After the first year there is no deadline for when notices must go out, and no tie to the assessment calendar.

5?
Exemptions

When is a sponsor's unit “sold” for purposes of losing its exemption?

The threshold question determines when previously exempt units enter the surcharge net.
