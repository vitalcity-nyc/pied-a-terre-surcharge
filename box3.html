What clear primary-residence rules should look like

The statute leaves “primary residence” largely to Finance's rulemaking. Fair rules would make any one of these conclusive.

Any one of the following should conclusively establish a primary residence — shifting the burden to the City to rebut:

  • The address on the owner's New York State resident income tax return.
  • A co-op or condo tax abatement certification, which already requires primary-residence use.
  • A STAR exemption or credit.
  • A documented one-year lease to a full-time tenant.

And one more path

For New Yorkers who live here but are domiciled elsewhere

Retirees who winter in warmer states, for instance, may not file a New York resident return even though their New York home is genuinely their primary residence. Comparable proof should count — otherwise the tax risks falling on people it was never meant to reach.
